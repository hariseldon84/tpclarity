# Glossary

*Note: Key terms are defined here and used consistently across the book.*

## Core Terms
- **Product Genome**: A structured framework serving as the DNA of a product; consists of Purpose DNA, User DNA, Experience DNA, and Architecture DNA.
- **Purpose DNA (The Intent)**: The non-negotiable mission defining why the product exists and its "Anti-Goals".
- **User DNA (The Problem)**: The psychological and functional profile of the target users and their pain points.
- **Experience DNA (The Interface)**: The logic of interaction focused on friction reduction.
- **Architecture DNA (The Structure)**: The technical constraints and logic for scalability and system development.
- **Clarity Gates**: 4 stages (Intent, Blueprint, Forge, Evolution) with strict thresholds that must be validated before moving forward.
- **MQB (Minimum Quality Bar)**: Non-negotiable standards that must be met before passing a Clarity Gate.
- **ADR (Architecture Decision Records)**: Documentation of the "Why" behind every technical choice.
- **The Skeleton MVP**: An anti-pattern describing shipping "anything" fast to save time, sacrificing quality and architecture, often leading to strategic failure.
- **System 1 Thinking**: Fast, automatic, emotional, stereotypic, and subconscious ways users process information and interact with software interfaces (Kahneman).
- **System 2 Thinking**: Slow, effortful, logical, calculating, and conscious ways product managers and developers design and build software (Kahneman).
- **Essential Complexity**: The inherent, unavoidable difficulty of solving a specific problem.
- **Accidental Complexity**: Unnecessary difficulty created by poor architecture, convoluted tools, or bad design choices.
- **Grandma's Closet**: A metaphorical parking lot for stakeholder ideas that do not align with the immediate 6-Month Impact Filter or Purpose DNA. Used to aggressively protect focus without discarding potentially valuable long-term ideas.
- **6-Month Impact Filter**: A conversation mechanism used to evaluate new ideas. If an idea does not directly impact the product launch in the next 6 months, it is sent to Grandma's Closet.
- **Organizational Entropy**: The thermodynamic chaos that happens in software development when teams lack an MQB framework to define the objective invariants of quality.
- **Anti-Goal**: An explicit declaration of what a product will *not* do, used to protect the Purpose DNA from scope creep.
- **Conway's Law**: The adage that systems and architectures mirror the communication structures of the organizations that design them (Conway, 1968).
- **Clarity Process API**: The concept that the Clarity Process does not replace Agile, but acts as a wrapper or "wafer layer" that integrates with existing sprints to ensure structural predictability.
- **Clarity Gate**: Strict, non-negotiable transition thresholds linking the stages of the product lifecycle. They operate as strict checklists rather than arbitrary management approvals.
- **Exceptional Process**: The formal documentation and executive sign-off required if a team actively chooses to bypass a Clarity Gate criterion, deliberately accepting technical debt.
- **Clarity Compass**: The master visual framework of the book. It consists of the 4 DNAs located in the protected Inner Core, governed by the 4-Stage Engine revolving in the Outer Ring.
- **Feature Factory**: A reactive product team that defaults to building every requested feature or copying competitors without underlying Purpose DNA, resulting in bloated, unfocused software.
- **The Mom Test**: A concept (from Rob Fitzpatrick) emphasizing the danger of asking users hypothetical questions; instead, product teams must observe actual behavior and constraints.
- **Inverse Conway Maneuver**: The act of intentionally restructuring the human communication lines of an organization (e.g., creating cross-functional squads) to force the software architecture to naturally heal and decouple.
